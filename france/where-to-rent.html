If you can find a short-term rental (1-3 months), then you can look with a real estate agent once you arrive in France. You may be able to find more of what you are looking for that way, but you will pay an additional agent fee.

Then there is the option of furnished or unfurnished. Unfurnished are usually rented without appliances and are often 3 year leases. There is a different set of tenant rights governing long-term rentals and it would be difficult to attain a long-term rental without a finacial history in France. If you choose to go this route, look for a emmaus, troc d'ile, or trocante outlet to find cheap, second-hand appliances. Furnished rentals will be more, but may be easier to rent without a finacial history in France. They are typically rented for a period of 12 months or less. They will not have the same legal protections as a long-tem rental.
